MIT Mystery Hunt 2026 Brain Dump

I was inspired to write this blog post because my team, Team Providence (this year named The Providence Bureau of Invest-Egg-Ations), won the MIT Mystery Hunt! Yay, that means we get to write next year’s hunt (which will also be lots of work!)

What is the MIT Mystery Hunt?

The MIT Mystery Hunt is one of the oldest and largest puzzle hunts in the world. It takes place annually over MLK weekend. I’m not going to say much more about what puzzle hunts are or what the MIT Mystery Hunt is because there’s already plenty of good content on all of this. This post isn’t mainly about the puzzles I worked on either (though there is some of that); it is more a diary entry of interesting things that I learned during the course of 2026’s hunt. In general, the hunt tends to draw in lots of interesting people who know trivia about lots of nerdy or esoteric topics.

I’ve been a casual solver (who loves going to Puzzled Pint regularly), and especially with a young kid now, I limited the amount of time I spent this year on the hunt. I probably thought about somewhere between 6 and 10 puzzles out of 200+ puzzles total. The two puzzles below are probably where I spent most of my mental energy though (and they were relatively challenging for the team as a whole).
